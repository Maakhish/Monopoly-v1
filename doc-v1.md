
# 🏆 Monopoly London – Base Version Rules

## 🎯 Objective

- Be the last player not bankrupt.
- Alternatively, the game ends when all properties have been purchased. In that case:
	- If at least one player has no loan from the Bank, that player wins.
	- If all remaining players have loans, the winner is determined by the Property Owned / Loan ratio (PO/L).
		- Example: Player A owns 10 properties with a loan of £2000 → ratio = 10/2000 = 0.005.
		- Player B owns 8 properties with a loan of £1000 → ratio = 8/1000 = 0.008.
		- Player B wins because 0.008 > 0.005.

## 🎲 Setup

- Each player starts with £1500.
- Banker manages money, properties, houses, hotels, and cards.
- Shuffle Chance (Orange Deck) and Community Chest (Yellow Deck), place them face down.
- Tokens start on GO.

## 🚶 Turn Flow

1. Roll two dice, move clockwise.
2. Follow the rules of the space you land on.
3. Doubles = extra turn; three doubles in a row = Jail.
4. End turn, pass dice left.

## 🏠 Properties & Buying Rules

- **First Lap Restriction:** Players cannot buy properties until they have completed one full lap (passed GO once).
- **After First Lap:**
	- If you land on an unowned property, you may buy it immediately.
	- If you don’t buy, it stays unowned (no auctions in this version).
- **Owned Property:** Pay rent if the owner asks before the next player rolls.

## 🏗 Buildings

- Once a property is bought, the owner may immediately place houses or a hotel on it.
- **Limits:**
	- Max 4 houses per property.
	- Max 1 hotel per property.
- **Color Set Bonus:**
	- If a player owns all properties of a color set, the rent from houses/hotels on those properties is doubled.
	- Example: A hotel on Old Kent Road normally earns £25. If the owner also owns Whitechapel Road (completing the Brown set), rent becomes £25 × 2 = £50.

## 💰 Money, Rent & Bankruptcy

- Rent is paid when landing on owned properties.
- **Railroads:** Rent increases with number owned.
- **Utilities:** Rent = dice roll × 4 (one utility) or × 10 (both).
- **No Property Trading Between Players.**
- **Get Out of Jail Free Card:**
	- Can be sold to the Bank for £1000.
	- When sold, the card is placed at the bottom of its original deck (Chance or Community Chest).
- **If you cannot pay:**
	- Sell houses/hotels back to the Bank (half cost).
	- Mortgage properties (collect mortgage value).
	- If still in debt → bankrupt.
		- Owe another player → give them all properties/cards.
		- Owe the Bank → properties auctioned, cards returned.

## 🚨 Special Spaces

- **GO:** Collect £200 when passing.
- **Chance / Community Chest:** Always active — draw and follow instructions, even during the first lap.
- **Income Tax / Luxury Tax:** Pay Bank.
- **Free Parking:** Nothing happens.
- **Jail:** Just visiting if landed. If sent, you must:
	- Pay £50, or
	- Use a Get Out of Jail Free card, or
	- Roll doubles within 3 turns (else pay £50).
- **Go To Jail:** Move directly to Jail, no £200 for passing GO.

## 🎨 Property Color Sets (London Board)

- **Brown:** Old Kent Road, Whitechapel Road
- **Light Blue:** The Angel Islington, Euston Road, Pentonville Road
- **Pink/Purple:** Pall Mall, Whitehall, Northumberland Avenue
- **Orange:** Bow Street, Marlborough Street, Vine Street
- **Red:** Strand, Fleet Street, Trafalgar Square
- **Yellow:** Leicester Square, Coventry Street, Piccadilly
- **Green:** Regent Street, Oxford Street, Bond Street
- **Dark Blue:** Park Lane, Mayfair
- **Stations (Black):** King’s Cross, Marylebone, Fenchurch Street, Liverpool Street
- **Utilities (White/Gray):** Electric Company, Water Works

## 📦 Chance Cards (Orange Deck)

- Advance to Go (Collect £200)
- Advance to Trafalgar Square (If you pass Go, collect £200)
- Advance to Marylebone Station (If you pass Go, collect £200)
- Advance token to the nearest Utility
- Advance token to the nearest Station (If unowned, you may buy it; if owned, pay owner double the rental)
- Bank pays you dividend of £50
- Go Back 3 Spaces
- Go to Jail (Go directly to Jail. Do not pass Go, do not collect £200)
- Make general repairs on all your property (For each house pay £25; For each hotel pay £100)
- Speeding fine £15
- Take a trip to King's Cross Station (If you pass Go, collect £200)
- Advance to Pall Mall (If you pass Go, collect £200)
- You have been elected Chairman of the Board (Pay each player £50)
- Your building loan matures (Collect £150)
- Advance to Mayfair
- Get Out of Jail Free (This card may be kept until needed or sold)

## 📦 Community Chest Cards (Yellow Deck)

- Bank error in your favour: Collect £200
- Annuity matures: Receive £100
- Life insurance matures: Collect £100
- You inherit: Receive £100
- From sale of stock you get: Receive £50
- Receive consultancy fee: Collect £25
- Income tax refund: Collect £20
- It is your birthday: Collect £10 from every player
- You have won second prize in a beauty contest: Collect £10
- Pay hospital fees: Pay £100
- Doctor's fee: Pay £50
- Pay school fees: Pay £50
- Advance to GO: Collect £200
- Go to Jail: Go directly to Jail, do not pass GO, do not collect £200
- Get Out of Jail Free: This card may be retained until needed or sold
- You are assessed for street repairs: Pay £40 per house and £115 per hotel owned

## 🏁 Winning Conditions

- **Bankruptcy Elimination:** Last player not bankrupt wins.
- **No More Properties Available:**
	- If a player has no loan, they win.
	- If all have loans, winner is determined by Property Owned / Loan ratio (PO/L).
